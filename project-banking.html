A Study in Banking Competition

Course: Financial Markets and Intermediaries | Institution: Maastricht University

A comprehensive empirical assessment of competition, collusive behaviors, and market power extraction in the Illinois banking sector between 1984 and 2020.

Project Overview

This empirical research paper evaluates the evolution of the Illinois banking market by exploring the degree of competition over nearly four decades. Using quarterly Call Reports sourced from Wharton Research Data Services (WRDS), the study investigates how rent extraction and market power shift across distinct regulatory eras and varying spatial dimensions (urban vs. rural MSAs).

Technical Approach

The analysis applies Empirical Industrial Organization (EIO) models, specifically leaning on the Critical Mass framework. The modularized pipeline includes a Two-Stage Least Squares (2SLS) Instrumental Variable regression to estimate Demand Elasticity, Mark-up Lerner Index calculations, and Two-way Fixed Effects estimation (feols) for geographic and temporal heterogeneity.

Tools: R, 2SLS, Fixed Effects (feols), EIO Frameworks, Geospatial Mapping (ggplot2, tidygeocoder).
