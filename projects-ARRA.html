Reassessing the ARRA Employment Multiplier: A Continuous Treatment Approach

Course: Panel Data Econometrics | Grade: 9.5/10 | Institution: Maastricht University

An original econometric research paper reassessing the employment effects of the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) using continuous treatment estimators.

Project Overview

This study completes a comprehensive reassessment of the employment effects that the ARRA spending policy carried across the fifty United States between February 2009 and December 2010. By leveraging the harmonized state-level dataset created by Chodorow-Reich (2019) alongside granular BLS and BEA data, the paper evaluates the evolutionary and compounding effects of the job multiplier.

Technical Approach

The analysis relies heavily on advanced panel data econometric techniques. The methodology progresses from pre-period parallel trends testing and baseline IV models to dynamic event studies applying the Callaway, Goodman-Bacon, and Sant'Anna (CGBS) continuous treatment Difference-in-Differences estimator.

Tools: R, Two-Way Fixed Effects (TWFE), 2SLS, CGBS Difference-in-Differences.
