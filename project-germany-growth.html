West Germany Postwar Growth: Human Capital & Immigration

Course: Macroeconomics I | Grade: 8.5/10 | Institution: Maastricht University

An econometric decomposition of West Germany’s remarkable postwar economic recovery, the “Wirtschaftswunder” (1950-1990), with focus on human capital and immigration.

Key Contributions

  • Constructed a harmonized historical dataset from 11 archival time series.
  • Developed and calibrated five alternative growth-accounting frameworks.
  • Implemented modern calibration procedures in Julia (1,000+ lines of code).

Impact

Provides new empirical evidence on the drivers of economic miracles, with implications for development economics and migration policy.

Tools: Julia (DataFrames.jl, GLM.jl), PlutoNotebook | Methods: Growth accounting, Productivity decomposition.
