Revaluation of "The China Syndrome"

Course: Empirical Econometrics I | Grade: 8.5/10 | Institution: Maastricht University

A comprehensive replication study of the seminal paper by Autor, Dorn, and Hanson, evaluating the local labor market effects of Chinese import competition in the United States.

Project Overview

This research paper rigorously replicates and evaluates the findings of "The China Syndrome" (2013). By reconstructing the original dataset and econometric frameworks, the project investigates how rising exposure to Chinese imports impacted manufacturing employment, population changes, and wages across US Commuting Zones (CZs) between 1990 and 2007.

Technical Approach

The analysis was entirely conducted in R, focusing on instrumental variable (IV) strategies. The core methodology involved implementing a Shift-Share Model to isolate exogenous trade shocks. The project successfully replicated the main tables of the original paper, including pre-period placebo tests and comprehensive working-age population status evaluations, ensuring robust standard errors handling.

Tools: R, Tidyverse, AER, lmtest, Shift-Share IV Models, Econometric Replication.
