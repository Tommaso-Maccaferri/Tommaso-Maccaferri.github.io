Biography & Research

About Me

I’m a quantitative economist passionate about understanding how economies grow and how large economic shocks affect real-world outcomes. My approach combines rigorous theoretical foundations with modern computational tools to tackle complex empirical questions.

Currently pursuing a Research Master’s at Maastricht University, I’m developing expertise in applied macroeconomics with focus on labor economics, monetary policy and banking, and economic history. I believe in the power of replication studies, transparent research, and making all code and data publicly available.

What Drives My Research

  • Economic Shocks Analyzing how trade and technology transform local labor markets.
  • Long-run Growth Identifying the structural drivers of productivity and regional development.
  • Human Capital Exploring the intersection of education, migration, and economic outcomes.

My Research Approach

  • Reproducibility Commitment to open science with all code and data hosted on GitHub.
  • Methodological Rigor Applying advanced causal inference and identification strategies.
  • Replication & Extension Rigorously testing influential theories to ensure robust findings.

Core Interests

Labor Economics: Trade shocks, local labor markets, and employment dynamics.
Monetary Policy: Financial markets and macroprudential regulation.
Economic History: Postwar reconstruction and historical growth accounting.
Applied Econometrics: Panel data, shift-share designs, and difference-in-differences.

Beyond Economics

My professional experience in hospitality and retail has taught me valuable lessons about teamwork and operational management. Furthermore, my international volunteer work in Jordan, Ethiopia, and Tanzania has given me perspective on global development challenges and the importance of community-based interventions.

When I’m not analyzing data, you might find me exploring new programming techniques, reading economic history, or discussing research ideas with fellow students and academics.
