About Me

I’m a quantitative economist passionate about understanding how economies grow and how large economic shocks affect real-world outcomes. My approach combines rigorous theoretical foundations with modern computational tools to tackle complex empirical questions.

Currently pursuing a Research Master’s at Maastricht University, I’m developing expertise in applied macroeconomics with focus on labor economics, monetary policy and banking, and economic history. I believe in the power of replication studies, transparent research, and making all code and data publicly available.

What Drives My Research

I’m particularly interested in questions at the intersection of macroeconomics and empirical microeconomics:

  • How do large economic shocks (trade, technology, migration) affect local labor markets?
  • What factors drive long-run economic growth and productivity?
  • How can we rigorously test and extend influential economic theories?
  • What role do human capital and migration play in economic development?

My Research Approach

I believe in:

  • Reproducible Research: All code and data publicly available on GitHub
  • Methodological Rigor: Careful attention to identification strategies and causal inference
  • Replication Studies: Testing robustness of influential papers and extending their findings
  • Clear Communication: Making complex econometric analysis accessible and transparent
  • Continuous Learning: Always exploring new methods, tools, and research questions

Beyond Economics

My professional experience in hospitality and retail has taught me valuable lessons about teamwork, customer service, and operational management. My international volunteer work in Jordan, Ethiopia, and Tanzania has given me perspective on global development challenges and the importance of community-based interventions.

When I’m not analyzing data or coding, you might find me exploring new programming techniques, reading economic history, following developments in labor economics and monetary policy, or discussing research ideas with fellow students and academics.
